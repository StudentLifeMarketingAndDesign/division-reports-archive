Office of the Sexual Misconduct Response Coordinator

A comprehensive prevention strategy requires multidisciplinary collaboration

Comprehensive prevention and education efforts are one component of a multi-faceted comprehensive strategy for ending gender-based discrimination and violence in our community. Many groups on campus are involved in prevention and education efforts, bringing diverse perspectives that reach a broader audience than any one group can achieve alone. The Campus Education Subcommittee brings together education providers and stakeholders from across campus to facilitate collaboration and ensure that messages are in alignment.

The UI’s education efforts begin prior to students arriving on campus with a mandatory online education program. All incoming undergraduate and transfer students are required to complete an online sexual misconduct prevention course called Every Choice, and all incoming graduate and professional students are required to take Not Anymore. The Every Choice and Not Anymore programs are coordinated by Student Health & Wellness. In addition to coordinating program implementation, monitoring completion, and providing support to students, Student Health & Wellness facilitates an alternate program option for students who unable to complete the online course due to personal experience with these issues.

Once arriving on campus, all incoming undergraduate students are required to attend the CHOOSE session during On Iowa! During the session students expand on what they learned in the online program, particularly on the topic of bystander intervention, by viewing a video illustrating various problematic situations staged on campus to show students intervening to make campus safer and more welcoming.

After orientation, students have opportunities to continue to participate in events related to the prevention of sexual misconduct, dating violence, and stalking. Post-orientation events are varied in their format, and include workshops, awareness raising campaigns, curriculum infusion, and community events. Collaboration between the departments responsible for providing education ensures that our messaging is aligned and that our programs build upon pre-orientation and orientation programming. In 2016, a total of 118 workshops were offered on topics including healthy relationships, bystander intervention, consent, risk reduction, awareness raising, and policy and crime information.

Departments involved in offering workshops included the Women’s Resource & Action Center (WRAC), RVAP, Student Health & Wellness, Student Legal Services, Department of Public Safety, OSMRC, Domestic Violence Intervention Program (DVIP), Department of Athletics, and Transformative Healing. "Collaboration has been integral to developing a comprehensive education model for students and a process to capture the many education efforts that are happening across campus,” says Tanya Villhauer, associate director for harm reduction and strategic initiatives and co-chair of the Campus Education Subcommittee. “It truly takes a village!”

Elizabeth Willmore
