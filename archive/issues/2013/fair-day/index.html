Fair Day a Positive Student Experience

Written byCaitlin Scott

The IMU hosted the first Fair Day on January 24, 2013 -- combining three previously-separate fairs, the Volunteer Fair, the Student Organization Fair, and the Housing Fair, into one enormous fair. Though there may have not been an actual tent in the IMU, the fair-inspired atmosphere could be felt throughout the entire building.

“The fair idea is really fun,” said Eric Rossow, Administrative Services Coordinator at the Center for Student Involvement and Leadership. ”Where else are you going to get cotton candy in Iowa City in January?”

The event featured snow-cones, cotton candy, hot chocolate, and airbrushed "trucker hats" free of charge. The Fair Day spirit energized students and created a lasting impression on many — especially the off-campus housing vendors, who experienced increased traffic to their booths according to Penny Kaelber, Coordinator for Campus Information.

“Our feedback from the evaluation that we gave each of the participating vendors was absolutely great,” Kaelber says. “The vendors commented with things like ‘we would love to come back, keep it part of the all fair’, because [attendance] numbers were a lot better.”

The Student Organization Fair, the Housing Fair, and the Volunteer Fair were previously held as separate events, which proved to be a particularly problematic format for the Housing Fair.

“There was lower attendance during the Housing Fair last year, and we just thought about what it would be like if we had all three [of the fairs] together because really wanted to drive traffic to each event,” Rossow says.

While the Volunteer Fair and the Student Organization Fair offered opportunities to engage with the community, the Housing Fair helps students physically move into the community. The UI Off-Campus Housing Service provides free lease review, resources for tenant-landlord issues, and assistance in finding roommates.

Rossow expects the new Fair Day format will continue in the future, citing positive feedback from Fair Day attendees. “We did a lot of planning and that really paid off,” Rossow says. 

“It’s just getting people down here to see what’s available and what is offered in this building is always an ongoing priority for all of us.”

Students were surveyed during Fair Day and, according to Rossow, the assessments point to positive experiences. 117 students were surveyed and responses indicated that students' expectations of Fair Day were met and that they preferred the multiple fair format. The majority of the attendees were sophomores and juniors, which Rossow thought was a promising statistic. “We are currently talking about hosting the Volunteer Fair, Organization Fair, and maybe the Campus Job Fair altogether in the fall,” Rossow said. “I think that we really like the concept [of the combined Fair Day] because it’s affordable and having it during the first syllabus week, [students] are learning about classes so they have a little more time to come to things.”

The Division of Student Life is continuously looking for ways to improve communication to students and Kaelber thinks that Fair Day is a step in the right direction.

“It’s just getting people down here to see what’s available and what is offered in this building is always an ongoing priority for all of us,” Kaelber says.
