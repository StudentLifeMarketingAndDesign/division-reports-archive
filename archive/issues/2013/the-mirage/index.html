Student Health & Wellness

Fostering Diversity and Inclusion

Written byHanna Rosman

When visiting Las Vegas, one might expect to see casino games, drag shows, or burlesque shows. Last year, all of these and more were part of the first annual Mirage event on campus. “The Mirage” is meant to entertain while educating students about AIDS.

Steph Beecher, a health educator at Student Health and Wellness, spearheaded the event when she saw a need for it on campus two years ago. The event development began when Beecher took a group of students on a “condom crawl” in Downtown Iowa City to promote sexual health.

The group attended a drag show at Studio 13, a local dance club. Beecher noticed that the students were nervous at first, but as the event carried on, they relaxed and enjoyed the atmosphere. The group ended up staying there for an hour when they were scheduled to be there only fifteen minutes. When debriefing with the students Beecher realized many could benefit from more exposure to the full spectrum of healthy sexuality.

The main goals of “The Mirage” were to promote diversity and inclusion in our community, raise awareness for World AIDS Day by providing free HIV testing, and provide information regarding sexual health, HIV/AIDS, and diversity.

Keeping students engaged during the entire event was a challenge. Nick Lopez, a senior majoring in health promotion, helped plan the event from its inception. Lopez and other organizers hoped students wouldn’t simply drop by the event to pick up free materials, but stay for the programming.

“It was awesome,” says Lopez. “Just seeing how much fun everybody else was having at the event made me happy because we put so much time and effort towards it.”

Keeping students engaged was achieved by planning a mix of activities, including educational tables at the Condom Casino, and amateur and professional drag shows. “The Mirage” included faculty and community members as well as students.

“You’re sitting next to a student, you’re sitting next to Tom Rocklin,” says Beecher. “It was really neat to have that diversity.”

Rocklin, VP of Student Life, not only attended but acted as one of the judges on the amateur drag show judge panel.

“I wanted to participate because I think it's a way of showing the university’s openness to people of all kinds of backgrounds and identities and different ways to express themselves,” says Rocklin. “I think that’s important.”

"I wanted to participate because I think it's a way of showing the university’s openness to people of all kinds of backgrounds and identities and different ways to express themselves"

Sexual health resources provided by Student Health & Wellness are often narrowly defined by their medical purpose. Most students think of these services as flat -- free condoms, HIV testing, or STI testing. "The Mirage" is one way that Student Health & Wellness breaks through that mindset.

“When I started this job as a full time sexual health educator, we wanted to expand the definition of what sexual health is,” says Beecher. “It’s not just using condoms, it’s more than that. It’s communication, it’s sexual identity... It’s not just for LGBT folks, it’s for straight people.”

“The Mirage” was held during Iowa City Red Week, which is a week of events that aims to reduce the stigma of HIV/AIDS and make testing more accessible. Free HIV/AIDS testing was offered at “The Mirage” to remove the fear of getting tested. Approximately 40 students and community members were tested during the event.

“You’d think it would be awkward, but it was neat in the sense that it was not abnormal,” says Beecher. “We want you do embrace your sexuality and we want you to get tested and not be afraid to be seen getting tested. We should applaud people who get tested.”

“The Mirage” is an event that will continue on an annual basis, and the planning committee aims to make it bigger and better for next year. Future goals include getting more contributions from local businesses, increasing attendance, and having the event run just as smoothly as last year.

Assessment of The Mirage

Prior to the amateur and professional performances, attendees took part in the Condom Casino by visiting various diversity and sexual health informational tables. Evaluations of the event indicated that participants learned useful skills and information that they will apply to live a healthier lifestyle. Students also reported that they will engage in healthy behaviors and encourage others to do the same.

100% of those who completed the evaluation reported that they felt “The Mirage” was a welcoming event. A student commented that they “felt more welcome than many places on campus” and that “people were friendly and were aware of pronoun usage”. Another student said, “I love drag shows! [It’s a] celebration of who people are”. For future events, a focus will be placed on getting more participants to complete the evaluation.
