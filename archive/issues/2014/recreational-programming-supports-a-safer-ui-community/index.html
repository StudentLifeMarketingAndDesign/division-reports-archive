Recreational programming supports a safer UI community

Written byAlisa Hrustic

The UI’s harm reduction plan guides our efforts to create a safer campus environment by decreasing the harms associated with high-risk alcohol use. One tactic within the plan is to expand late night programming via Recreational Services to provide more options for alcohol-free events and activities.  

The Recreational Services staff determined that one-time events versus those requiring more commitment were more likely to attract students who may be deciding, at that moment, what to do for entertainment. In this regard, the programming could also serve as an introduction to Recreational Services for students who have never participated in any Rec Services activities or events.

A variety of activities including kickball, inner tube water polo, and bowling were planned. One of the most innovative activities, Canoe Battleship, was held near finals week. Canoe Battleship involves teams of 3-4 people and several canoes. The game’s goal is to sink the opposing teams’ canoes by filling the canoe with water.

During the 2013 event, 30 teams composed of 117 people took part in Canoe Battleship. 40% of those involved reported that Canoe Battleship was their first time participating in an organized Recreational Services activity at Iowa. Freshmen students formed the highest portion of participants at 35%. 

About a quarter of students who attended said they would have been participating in an activity involving alcohol if they hadn’t come to Canoe Battleship. Another 60% reported they would have been hanging out with friends.

Activities for 2014-2015 will include Canoe Battleship, Wallyball (volleyball in a racquetball court), and bowling, along with seven other late night events.  This programming continues to serve a population of students who benefit from late night options that do not revolve around alcohol use.
