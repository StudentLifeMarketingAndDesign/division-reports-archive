SCOPE members learn new skills while bringing live music to campus

Written byEmily Nolan

SCOPE Productions, the UI's Student Commission on Programming Entertainment, provides students with real-world experience in the music production and performance industry through facilitating musical and entertainment productions of all sizes, for the benefit of UI students and the broader community. 

SCOPE is governed by a board of students that direct committees in marketing, public relations, hospitality, production, and finance. SCOPE adds to the local cultural scene as well as provides the opportunity for individuals to explore new music in a live setting. Students work hard to book a diverse lineup of artists in a wide variety of genres at an affordable ticket price for students and the public. 

Becoming a member of SCOPE is rewarding for students; they create a memorable experience of live music for their peers and the public while also gaining experience in the field of music.

Dan Rowley, the production director of SCOPE, says that being involved in the organization has not only been a fun way to get involved on campus, but has also led him to choose arts management as his future career field.

Learn more about SCOPE and see an upcoming shows listing on their website.
